Saturday Wedding


Timezone

Grand Canyon is in Mountain Standard Time (MST).
Grand Canyon, AZ and Flagstaff, AZ are in a different timezone from Kayenta, AZ.

Park Fees

Admission to Grand Canyon National Park is for seven days and includes both the North Rim and South Rim.

Grand Canyon National Park Vehicle Permit - $35 (U.S. Dollars)
Admits one single, private, non-commercial vehicle and all its passengers, up to a 15 person passenger van. Organized groups are not eligible for the vehicle permit.

For more information about more types of fees or more details about the fees please visit Grand Canyon Fee Information

Parking

Parking is available at the church for both the ceremony and the reception.

Electronics

Please turn off your cellphones and wait to take photos until after the wedding ceremony. Thank you!

Snack/Cocktail Hour

During the time between 12:00 PM - 2:00 PM you can enjoy the snacks we have prepared for you and you can roam around the south rim of the grand canyon.

Timezone

Direction Information

The Diné (Navajo) ceremony will be located outside of Kayenta, AZ on my family homestead. The location is kind of hard to navigate to so we are planning to have the guest follow a driver to the location. The road to the ceremony location starts on paved road but most of the drive is on a dirt road. If you are in cars that are kind of lower than a truck, we will take our time to drive to the location to keep everyone cars safe.

As of 08/19/2020, we are still making the arrangements. Please come back for more updated information in the coming weeks.

Parking

Parking is available at the ceremony location. Parking is unpaved.

Electronics

During the Diné ceremony, there will be no photography or audio recording allowed. Therefore phones and cameras are not allowed during the ceremony for it is sacred.

Weather

Weather near Kayenta can be a little chilly depending on the year. In 2019 the weather was around 60 - 72 degrees as the high.

Shoe Recommendation

Since the ceremony is mostly outdoors we would recommend you to wear close toed shoes unless you like feeling pebbles and sticker weeds in your shoes.

Local Parks

Currently as of 08/19/2020, the Monument Valley park and the National Navajo Monument is closed due to COVID-19. Please visit the Monument Valley website and Navajo National Monument for more information.

We have 2 locations you can visit if you want to do some sightseeing before the wedding or anytime you are visiting.

Monument Valley is North of Kayenta, AZ. The drive to Monument Valley is around a 30 minute drive (28 miles) from Kayenta. Navajo National Monument is west of Kayenta, AZ. If you are driving from Flagstaff to Kayenta. The monument is on the drive before you arrive in Kayenta. You can enjoy the nature walk or view the Pueblo cliff village.
